BIO

As a designer, I couldn’t have been luckier than to have been a child during the 90’s animation renaissance; an era of exploration and imagination. When shows and movies took risks in developing living art. From the backgrounds in Aladdin, to the stylings of Batman, or the transformations in Sailor Moon, I wanted nothing more than to create something just as amazing.

I was inspired and I couldn’t get a hold of art supplies fast enough to keep up with every new project I just had to make.This endless need to create leading me to continuously volunteer throughout my years in grade school. I began making shirt designs for my youth group evolving into requests for, event signs for community service and, my favorite, playbills for my school and community drama productions. Prior to Adobe, I either created everything by hand or used programs like Microsoft Paint or Publisher to produce my designs.

I loved it.

I spent hours on each of my creations, meticulously making sure every detail was perfect and yet I had no clue that the art I was creating was actually design. It wasn’t until I was given two major options for college law or graphic design, that a light bulb went off inside my head. It was an epiphany. I was a designer and that’s what I wanted to be. I chose the graphic design college pamphlet and never looked back.

To this day, I continue to learn what it means to be a designer and to evolve my skills, challenging myself with new and exciting projects. But when I’m seeking inspiration, I always return to the animations that sparked it in me to begin with.
